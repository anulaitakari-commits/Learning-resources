Content Development Tools — Comparison Guide

A practical reference for tools you can use to create, present and publish digital learning content. Filter by category or cost. Tools marked "Know about" are industry-standard but require institutional or paid access — worth being aware of professionally.

⚡ Accessibility ratings reflect each tool's built-in features and output quality — not a guarantee that every piece of content you create will be accessible. Good accessibility always requires deliberate design choices regardless of the tool.

H5P Interactive content

Creates interactive learning content — quizzes, drag-and-drop, timelines, branching scenarios, interactive video — that embeds directly into Moodle and most VLEs.

Cost

Free (h5p.org) Freemium (H5P.com)

Accessibility

Keyboard navigable Screen reader support Moodle native Alt text: manual

Best for

Formative quizzes Interactive video Branching scenarios
Free at h5p.org. If your institution runs Moodle, H5P is likely already installed. Check with your LMS admin before creating an account elsewhere.
Genially Interactive content

Creates interactive presentations, infographics, escape rooms and image hotspots. More visual and flexible than H5P — better for storytelling and immersive content.

Cost

Free tier available

Accessibility

Limited screen reader Alt text: manual Not always keyboard-only

Best for

Visual explainers Interactive infographics Escape rooms
Strong visual results but accessibility limitations mean it works best as a supplementary resource alongside accessible alternatives — not as a sole delivery method.
Canva Visual & design

Drag-and-drop design tool for slides, infographics, posters, social media assets and short video. Free education accounts available. Widely used for learning material design.

Cost

Free (education) Pro tier available

Accessibility

Accessibility checker built in Colour contrast tool PDF export: partial tags Alt text: manual

Best for

Visual resources Slide decks Infographics Thumbnails
Apply for a free Canva for Education account at canva.com/education. Use the built-in accessibility checker before exporting any content.
Padlet Collaboration

Digital bulletin board for collaborative content collection, discussion and curation. Students can post text, images, links and video. Good for low-stakes collaborative activities.

Cost

Free (3 boards) Paid for more

Accessibility

Basic keyboard nav Screen reader: limited Alt text: manual

Best for

Collaborative boards Resource sharing Formative activities
The free tier limits you to three boards simultaneously — archive old ones to stay within the limit. Consider whether the discussion board in your VLE would serve the same purpose more accessibly.
Loom Video & screen

Screen and camera recording tool for creating short instructional videos, walkthroughs and feedback recordings. Generates an automatic transcript and shareable link instantly.

Cost

Free (education) Business tier available

Accessibility

Auto transcript generated Captions available Caption accuracy: review needed

Best for

Lecturecasts Feedback recording Walkthroughs
Always review and correct auto-generated captions before sharing. Free for educators at loom.com/education. If your institution has Panopto, use that instead for better VLE integration.
Google Sites Authoring & LMS

Free website builder for creating structured learning resource hubs, module guides and portfolio sites. No coding required. Embeds Google Docs, Slides, Forms and YouTube natively.

Cost

Free (Google account)

Accessibility

Semantic HTML output Keyboard navigable Alt text: manual Colour contrast: check manually

Best for

Resource hubs Module guides Student portfolios
A practical choice for creating a lightweight companion site alongside a VLE — useful if your institution's LMS is restrictive. Check institutional data policies before hosting learner-facing content.
Panopto Video & screen

Institutional video platform for recording, hosting and streaming lectures and screencasts. Integrates natively with Moodle and most VLEs. Includes auto-captioning and transcript search.

Cost

Institutional licence

Accessibility

Auto captions Transcript search VLE integrated Caption accuracy: review needed

Best for

Lecturecasts Recorded sessions Video libraries
If your institution has Panopto, use it in preference to Loom for anything that will be stored and accessed long-term. Always review auto-captions before publishing.
Articulate Rise Authoring & LMS

Browser-based e-learning authoring tool producing responsive, SCORM-compatible modules. Industry standard in corporate L&D. Produces polished, accessible courses without coding.

Cost

Know about — paid

Accessibility

WCAG 2.1 AA compliant output Keyboard navigable Screen reader tested

Best for

SCORM courses Corporate L&D Responsive modules
You are unlikely to access Rise freely but it is the dominant tool in corporate content development. Understanding what it produces helps you speak the language of professional content studios.
Articulate Storyline Authoring & LMS

Desktop e-learning authoring tool for building complex, branching SCORM modules with custom interactions. More powerful and more demanding than Rise — used by specialist content developers.

Cost

Know about — paid

Accessibility

SCORM/xAPI output Accessibility: requires deliberate setup Screen reader: depends on build

Best for

Complex branching Custom interactions Simulations
Storyline has a 60-day free trial. If you work in corporate L&D or are moving into a dedicated content development role, it is worth exploring. Accessibility in Storyline requires intentional effort — it is not automatic.
Microsoft Sway Visual & design

Microsoft's web-based presentation and report tool. Creates scrollable, responsive content that works well on any device. Integrates with Microsoft 365. Often overlooked but very accessible out of the box.

Cost

Free (Microsoft account)

Accessibility

Accessibility checker built in Responsive by default Keyboard navigable

Best for

Learning guides Visual reports Unit introductions
If your institution uses Microsoft 365 — which most UK universities do — you already have Sway. It is significantly more accessible than Genially and requires no additional account.
Mentimeter Collaboration

Live polling and audience response tool. Word clouds, multiple choice polls, open questions and Q&A — students respond on their own device in real time. Good for live session interaction.

Cost

Free (limited slides) Education discount available

Accessibility

Keyboard nav: partial Screen reader: limited Mobile responsive

Best for

Live polls Word clouds Live Q&A
Best used as a live session tool rather than asynchronous content. The free tier allows two questions per presentation — sufficient for most live session activities.
Wakelet Collaboration

Content curation and collection tool. Students and tutors can save links, videos, images and text into shared collections. Good for building collaborative reading lists or resource banks.

Cost

Free

Accessibility

Keyboard navigable Screen reader: partial Mobile responsive

Best for

Curated reading lists Resource banks Connectivist activities
A natural fit for connectivist learning activities where learners contribute to a shared knowledge network. Free with no content limits.

A note on tool choice No tool is inherently good or bad — the question is always whether it serves your learners' needs in your context. Before adopting any tool, ask: Is it accessible to all my learners? Does it work within my institution's data and privacy policies? Does it genuinely improve the learning experience, or does it add complexity without pedagogical benefit? The best tool is often the one your learners already know how to use.
